Unlike China where you can simply walk into a China Mobile (中国移动), China Unicom (中国联通), or China Telecom (中国电信) store and get a SIM card with your ID in minutes, the USA system can be confusing. This guide will help you set up the perfect temporary phone solution for your visiting parents, including how to keep their China Mobile number active while they're here.

## Why Regular USA Phone Plans Don't Work Well for Chinese Visitors

Here's what makes the typical American carrier stores frustrating for Chinese parents:

- **Language barriers**: Staff at Verizon, AT&T retail stores rarely speak Mandarin or Cantonese
- **Credit checks**: Postpaid plans require SSN and US credit history your parents don't have
- **Long contracts**: Most tourist visits are 2-6 months, but carriers push annual plans
- **Complicated activation**: Online-only activations confuse older generations not familiar with English interfaces
- **No international calling**: Basic plans don't include affordable calls to China (¥2-3/minute is common)
- **Expensive**: $50-70/month plans when your parents mostly just need WeChat and occasional calls

What your parents actually need is different from what American carriers assume.

A: Yes! WeChat works perfectly on Nexitel. They can video call family in China, post Moments, send red packets (红包), everything. Data speeds are fast enough for smooth video calls.
