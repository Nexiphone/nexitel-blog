## During the Emergency: Maximizing Your Service

When disaster hits, smart usage becomes critical. Network congestion often makes normal communication difficult, but these strategies help:

### Conserve Battery Life

Every percentage point of battery matters during extended outages:

1. **Activate Low Power Mode** immediately
2. **Reduce screen brightness** to minimum
3. **Close unnecessary apps** running in background
4. **Disable automatic updates** and background refresh
5. **Turn off Bluetooth, WiFi, and location** when not needed
6. **Use airplane mode** between essential communications

### Prioritize Text Over Calls

SMS messages use minimal bandwidth and often get through when voice calls fail:

- Text messages queue and send automatically when service returns
- They consume significantly less battery than voice calls
- Group texts efficiently update multiple people
- Carriers prioritize text traffic during congestion

### Leverage WiFi Calling When Available

If you reach a location with WiFi (shelters, emergency centers, cafes with generators), enable WiFi calling:

## Special Considerations for Different Disasters

Different emergencies require different strategies:

### Hurricanes and Tropical Storms

- Top up accounts 72 hours before landfall
- Download offline maps and evacuation routes
- Screenshot important documents and contacts
- Charge all devices fully before power loss

### Wildfires

- Enable emergency alerts for evacuation orders
- Keep phones in waterproof bags to prevent smoke damage
- Have car chargers ready for evacuation
- Pre-program evacuation center locations

### Floods

- Waterproof phone cases are essential
- Know which towers are elevated vs. ground-level
- Keep phones above flood levels
- Have waterproof storage for backup devices

### Winter Storms and Ice

- Cold drains batteries faster—keep phones warm
- Have multiple charging options (power often fails)
- Indoor antennas may help with ice-damaged towers
- Prepare for extended outages (days, not hours)
