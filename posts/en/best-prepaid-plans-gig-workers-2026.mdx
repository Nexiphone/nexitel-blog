## How Much Data Do Gig Workers Actually Need?

This is one of the most common questions, and the answer depends on how many hours you work and how many apps you run:

- **Navigation apps** — Google Maps uses roughly 5-10 MB per hour of active navigation
- **Gig platform apps** — each app uses about 100-200 MB per day with moderate use
- **Music streaming** — if you listen while driving, expect 50-150 MB per hour depending on quality settings
- **Communication** — calls and texts use minimal data, but in-app messaging and photo sharing add up

A full-time gig worker who drives 30-40 hours per week typically uses between 5 GB and 15 GB of data per month. Part-time drivers can often get by with less. Check [Nexitel's plans page](https://nexitel.us/plans) to find the tier that matches your usage.

## Tips to Maximize Your Plan While Gigging

Smart data habits help you stretch your plan further and keep costs low:

- **Download offline maps** — Google Maps lets you save maps for offline use, reducing data consumption during navigation
- **Use Wi-Fi at home** — update apps, download playlists, and sync data while on your home network
- **Close background apps** — social media and streaming apps consume data even when you are not using them
- **Monitor your usage** — check your data consumption weekly so you know whether to adjust your plan
- **Keep a backup plan** — if your primary phone dies, having a second device with a cheap Nexitel line means you never miss a shift
