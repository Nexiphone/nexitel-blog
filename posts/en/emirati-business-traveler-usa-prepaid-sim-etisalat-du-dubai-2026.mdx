## Common Issues and Solutions

### "My Nexitel SIM Isn't Connecting"

- Restart your phone after inserting the SIM
- Check that you're in a coverage area (major cities always work)
- Ensure airplane mode is OFF
- Try manual network selection: AT&T (Blue) or T-Mobile (Purple)
- Wait 5-10 minutes—sometimes activation takes time

### "I Can't Make International Calls"

- Verify international calling is enabled on your Nexitel account
- Use NexiTalk app instead for better rates to UAE
- Check you've dialed correctly: +971 (UAE code) then number
- Ensure you have account balance for per-minute charges

### "Data Isn't Working"

- Go to Settings → Cellular/Mobile Data
- Create new APN settings if prompted (Nexitel provides these)
- Toggle Cellular Data off and back on
- Verify you haven't exceeded your data allowance
- Try switching between 4G/LTE and 5G settings

### "My Etisalat/du SIM Is Roaming and Charging Me"

- Put Etisalat/du SIM in airplane mode
- Disable "Data Roaming" for that SIM specifically
- Remove the SIM if you don't need it for OTPs
- Contact Etisalat/du to deactivate roaming (do this before travel)

## Pro Tips from Experienced UAE Business Travelers

### Tip #1: Get the SIM Early
Order your Nexitel SIM 2-3 weeks before travel. Ship to a US colleague if needed, or to your first hotel. Having it ready eliminates airport SIM card hassles.

### Tip #2: Download Offline Maps
Before leaving WiFi, download offline Google Maps for your destination cities. Saves data and works without connection.

### Tip #3: Use Hotel WiFi Strategically
Save your Nexitel data for mobile use. Use hotel WiFi for large downloads, software updates, and streaming.

### Tip #4: Schedule Video Calls During WiFi Times
Use hotel or conference WiFi for long video calls to Dubai. Save Nexitel data for emails and messaging throughout the day.

### Tip #5: Keep Both SIMs Active (If Dual-SIM)
Modern iPhones and Samsung Galaxy phones support dual-SIM. Keep your UAE number available for receiving (in airplane mode to prevent charges), while using Nexitel for everything else.

### Tip #6: Test Everything at Your Hotel First
Before heading to meetings, verify calls, data, and apps all work properly from your hotel room. Troubleshoot with good WiFi backup available.
