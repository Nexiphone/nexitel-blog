## What to Look for in a Road Trip Phone Plan

Before loading up the car, make sure your wireless plan checks these boxes:

- **Nationwide coverage** — your plan should work across state lines, not just in your home city
- **Strong rural signal** — highways between cities pass through areas where only certain networks reach
- **Enough data for navigation** — GPS apps like Google Maps and Waze use mobile data continuously
- **Hotspot capability** — passengers may need Wi-Fi for entertainment or work
- **No overage charges** — predictable pricing no matter how far you drive
- **Month-to-month flexibility** — upgrade your data tier for the trip, then scale back when you return

## How Much Data Do You Need for a Road Trip?

Data usage on the road adds up faster than most people expect. Here is a rough breakdown:

- **GPS navigation** — about 50-100 MB per day of driving
- **Music streaming** — around 75 MB per hour
- **Video streaming (passengers)** — 1 to 3 GB per hour depending on quality
- **General browsing and social media** — 200-500 MB per day
- **Hotspot for a laptop** — varies widely, but budget at least 1 GB per day

For a week-long road trip with moderate use, plan for at least 10 GB of data. If passengers will stream video, consider an unlimited tier from Nexitel to avoid any concerns.

## Tips for Staying Connected on the Road

Even with the best plan, road trips can take you through challenging coverage areas. These tips help you stay connected:

1. **Download offline maps** — Google Maps and Apple Maps both let you save map regions for offline use
2. **Carry a portable charger** — your phone works harder searching for signal in weak areas, draining the battery faster
3. **Enable Wi-Fi calling** — when you stop at a hotel or restaurant with Wi-Fi, your calls route through the internet instead of cell towers
4. **Check coverage maps before your route** — Nexitel's website shows network coverage so you can plan accordingly
5. **Consider a dual-SIM setup** — use both a Blue and Purple plan to switch networks based on which has better signal in your area
