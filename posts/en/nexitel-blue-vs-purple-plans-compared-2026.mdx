## Network Coverage: Where Each One Shines

### Nexitel Blue (AT&T network)
AT&T has one of the largest LTE footprints in the country. If your commute takes you through rural highways, small towns, or mountain areas, Blue Plans tend to hold signal in places where other networks drop off. Blue is also a strong pick for travelers who road trip a lot or live in the Southeast, Texas, and the rural Midwest.

### Nexitel Purple (T-Mobile network)
T-Mobile has invested heavily in 2.5 GHz mid-band 5G, and it shows in major cities. If you live somewhere like New York, Los Angeles, Seattle, Miami, Chicago, or Dallas, Purple Plans often deliver faster 5G speeds than any other prepaid option. The tradeoff is that coverage in some rural corners still lags AT&T.

## 5G Speed Comparison

- **Blue (AT&T):** Rock-solid 5G in most urban and suburban areas with steady speeds for streaming, video calls, and browsing. Low-band 5G reaches further, making it great for consistent performance on the move.
- **Purple (T-Mobile):** Mid-band 5G (what T-Mobile calls Ultra Capacity) can reach peak speeds that feel like home Wi-Fi. If you upload video, game on mobile, or tether a laptop, Purple often wins on raw speed in covered cities.
