## The Challenge: Traditional Carriers and International Living

Major US carriers like Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile offer international roaming, but these plans come with serious drawbacks for long-term expats:

- **High monthly costs**: Premium plans cost $70-100/month even if you rarely use US services
- **Roaming fees**: International roaming adds $10-15/day in many destinations
- **Service suspension**: Many carriers will suspend or terminate your line after 2-3 months of continuous international use
- **Data limitations**: Roaming data is often throttled or capped at slow speeds
- **Contract requirements**: Postpaid plans lock you into long-term commitments

For someone living abroad full-time, these options simply don't make financial sense.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Don't Let Your Plan Expire

Set calendar reminders and enable auto-pay. If your prepaid plan expires, you may lose your number permanently after 30-60 days of inactivity.

### Don't Rely Solely on Data Apps

While WhatsApp, Telegram, and similar apps are great for communication, they don't help with:
- SMS-based verification codes
- Calls from US businesses that don't use VoIP
- Emergency services contact
- Account recovery systems

### Don't Ignore WiFi Calling Setup

Without WiFi calling enabled, you'll have limited ability to use your US number abroad. Configure this before leaving the US and test it thoroughly.

### Don't Use Roaming for Data

Even with a maintained US number, don't enable cellular data roaming internationally. The charges can be extreme. Use WiFi or a local SIM for data needs.
