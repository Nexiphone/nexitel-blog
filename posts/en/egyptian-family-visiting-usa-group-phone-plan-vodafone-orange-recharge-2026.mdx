Egyptian travelers face unique connectivity challenges that tourists from other countries don't experience:

**The Roaming Trap**: Activating international roaming with Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt, or WE will cost you 50-100 EGP per day (sometimes more), which adds up to thousands of pounds for a two-week family trip. For a family of four, you're looking at potentially 10,000+ EGP just for basic connectivity.

**Airport SIM Confusion**: Buying SIM cards at JFK, LAX, or O'Hare airports means paying premium tourist prices—often $40-60 per person for plans that run out halfway through your trip.

**Family Coordination**: When you're traveling with parents, children, and perhaps elderly relatives who need to reach each other constantly, you need reliable group connectivity that doesn't break the budget.

**Staying Connected to Egypt**: You still need to call family members back home, check on your property, coordinate with neighbors, or handle banking matters—but international calling rates from USA can be shockingly expensive.

### Handling Dual SIM Phones

Most modern smartphones from Egypt (especially Samsung, Xiaomi, Realme, OPPO) have dual SIM slots:

**Best Strategy**:
- **SIM 1**: Nexitel SIM for USA connectivity
- **SIM 2**: Keep your Vodafone/Orange/WE SIM active (turn OFF roaming) for receiving verification codes via SMS

This way you can still receive bank OTPs, WhatsApp verification, and important messages from Egypt without paying roaming fees—just make sure data roaming is disabled on the Egyptian SIM.

### Staying Safe and Connected

When traveling as a family in an unfamiliar country:

1. **Share locations** — Use WhatsApp Live Location or Google Maps location sharing so everyone knows where family members are
