## Why Streaming Changes the Plan You Need

If your phone is your primary TV, you are in a different category of mobile user. A 30-minute HD episode burns roughly 1 GB of data. A two-hour movie in full HD can chew through 3 to 4 GB. Stream daily over 5G and you can easily pass 40 GB in a month without realizing it.

Plans marketed to light users with 2 GB or 5 GB caps are a trap for streamers. Once you hit the cap, your video drops to 480p or worse, or the plan cuts off entirely. Heavy streamers need real high-speed allowances and clear throttling rules.

## What Heavy Streamers Should Look For

- Large high-speed 5G allowance, ideally 25 GB+ or unlimited
- Clear, transparent throttling policy after the cap
- Hotspot for casting to a tablet or laptop
- Access to both AT&T and T-Mobile 5G networks

## Tips to Get More Out of Every Gigabyte

### Adjust In-App Video Quality

Netflix, YouTube, and Instagram all default to the highest quality your bandwidth allows. Lock them to 720p when you are not on Wi-Fi. You get the same watchable quality for roughly half the data.

### Download for Offline

Almost every major streaming app now supports downloads. Load your phone up on home Wi-Fi before a commute and you will barely touch your monthly data.

### Turn Off Autoplay

Silent video autoplay in social feeds is a hidden data drain. Turn it off in Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter/X settings to save 1-3 GB per month.

## What About True Unlimited?

Most prepaid unlimited plans are not truly unlimited. They offer a "premium" high-speed portion (say, 50 GB), then deprioritize traffic for the rest of the month.
