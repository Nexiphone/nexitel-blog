## Keeping Your 中華電信 Number Active While Traveling

Many Taiwanese worry: "If I remove my Chunghwa SIM card, will I lose my number?" The answer is no—but you need to plan correctly.

### Before You Leave Taiwan

1. **Check your contract status**: If you're on a postpaid plan with 中華電信, your number stays active as long as you pay monthly bills
2. **Enable international roaming briefly**: You might want it for the airport arrival/departure
3. **Set up automatic bill payment**: Use credit card auto-pay or bank transfer so your Chunghwa bill gets paid while you're away
4. **Port your number to a cheaper plan**: Consider downgrading to a basic NT$199-299 plan just to keep the number alive

### Dual SIM Strategy for Modern Phones

Most newer smartphones (iPhone 12 and later, Samsung Galaxy S21+, Google Pixel 6+) support dual SIM—one physical SIM plus one eSIM. This is PERFECT for Taiwanese visitors:

**Slot 1 (Primary)**: Nexitel USA SIM for data, local calls, navigation
**Slot 2 (eSIM or secondary)**: Keep your Chunghwa SIM active for receiving important Taiwan bank/government messages

Set your phone to:
- Use Nexitel for cellular data
- Keep Chunghwa for SMS reception only (no data roaming to avoid charges)
- Route all calls through Nexitel or use NexiTalk for calling Taiwan

This way, you maintain your Taiwan number for verification codes but avoid roaming charges.
