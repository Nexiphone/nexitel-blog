## Your Phone Number Can Come With You

One of the biggest reasons people hesitate to switch from a contract carrier to prepaid service is fear of losing their phone number. The good news is that federal law protects your right to keep your number when you change carriers. It is called number portability, and it works with every major prepaid provider — including [Nexitel](https://nexitel.us/plans).

Whether you are moving from Verizon, AT&T postpaid, T-Mobile, or any other carrier, this guide walks you through the exact steps to port your number to a Nexitel Blue or Purple plan without any downtime or complications.

## What Is Number Porting?

Number porting is the process of transferring your existing phone number from one wireless carrier to another. The FCC requires all carriers to support this process, and it typically completes within a few hours to two business days.

Key facts about number porting:

- **It is free** — carriers cannot charge you a porting fee
- **It works across all carrier types** — postpaid to prepaid, MVNO to MVNO, landline to mobile
- **Your old account closes automatically** — once the port completes, your previous carrier releases the line
- **You keep the same number** — area code and all, nothing changes for the people who call you

## What You Need Before You Start

Gather this information from your current carrier before initiating a port:

1. **Your account number** — find this on your bill or in your carrier's app
2. **Your account PIN or password** — the security PIN you set when opening the account
3. **The name on the account** — must match exactly
4. **Your billing address** — the address your current carrier has on file
5. **Your phone number** — the number you want to transfer

### Where to Find Your Account Number

- **Verizon**: My Verizon app → Account → Account PIN & Security
- **AT&T**: myAT&T app → Account → Manage account
- **T-Mobile**: T-Mobile app → Account → Profile settings
- **Other carriers**: Call customer service or check your latest bill

If you want to bring your own device, make sure it is unlocked and compatible with your chosen network:

- **Purple Plans (T-Mobile network)**: Needs T-Mobile-compatible bands, including Band 71 for best 5G coverage

### Step 3: Activate Your Nexitel Plan With Your Existing Number

During the activation process, select the option to port an existing number. Enter:

- Your current phone number
- Your account number from your old carrier
- Your account PIN
- The name and address on your old account

### Step 4: Wait for the Port to Complete

Most ports complete within 2 to 4 hours during business days. During this window:

- **Do not cancel your old service** — the port process handles the cancellation automatically
- **Your phone may lose signal briefly** — this is normal during the switchover
- **Restart your phone** — once you receive confirmation, restart to activate the new network

### Step 5: Verify Everything Works

After the port completes, test the following:

- Make an outbound call
- Receive an inbound call
- Send and receive text messages
- Confirm mobile data is working

## Common Porting Problems and How to Fix Them

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Port rejected | Incorrect account info | Double-check account number and PIN with old carrier |
| Port delayed | Carrier processing backlog | Wait 24-48 hours; contact Nexitel support if longer |
| No service after port | SIM not activated | Restart phone and check APN settings |
| Texts not arriving | Short code routing delay | Wait 24 hours; routing updates propagate automatically |

## Important Timing Tips

- **Do not cancel your old plan first** — let the port process handle the transition
- **Port before your billing cycle renews** — avoid paying for an extra month with your old carrier
- **Keep your old SIM until the port confirms** — you may need it if there is a delay
- **Port on a weekday** — business-hour ports tend to complete faster
