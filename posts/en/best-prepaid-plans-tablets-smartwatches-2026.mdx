## Why Your Tablet and Smartwatch Deserve Their Own Plan

Smartphones are not the only devices that need cellular connectivity anymore. Tablets, smartwatches, GPS trackers, and other gadgets increasingly rely on mobile data to stay useful when Wi-Fi is unavailable. The problem is that most carriers push expensive add-on lines or require you to bundle them with a postpaid phone plan.

Prepaid plans solve that problem. You get a standalone data line with no contract, no credit check, and no surprise fees. Whether you are adding LTE to a tablet for a road trip or keeping a cellular smartwatch connected during runs, the right prepaid plan keeps costs predictable.

## What to Look For in a Device Plan

Before choosing a plan for a secondary device, consider these factors:

1. **Data-only or low-data tiers** — tablets and watches rarely need unlimited data
2. **Network compatibility** — check whether your device supports AT&T or T-Mobile bands
3. **No contract commitment** — activate when you need it, pause when you do not
4. **eSIM support** — many modern tablets and watches use eSIM exclusively
5. **Affordable monthly cost** — secondary devices should not cost as much as your phone line

Nexitel offers plans starting at just $6 per month, which makes it realistic to add connectivity to multiple devices without breaking the bank.

### How Much Data Do You Actually Need?

Most secondary devices use surprisingly little data:

- **Smartwatch**: 200-500 MB per month for notifications, calls, and fitness syncing
- **Tablet (light use)**: 1-3 GB per month for email, browsing, and maps
- **Tablet (streaming)**: 5-10 GB per month if you watch video regularly
- **GPS tracker**: under 100 MB per month
