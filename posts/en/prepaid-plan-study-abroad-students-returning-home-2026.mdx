Congratulations on completing your study abroad journey! Whether you spent a semester in Barcelona, a year in Seoul, or studied across multiple countries, returning to the United States means getting your phone service sorted out quickly. The good news? - **Instant activation** — get your US number working within minutes of landing
- **Budget-friendly pricing** — keep costs low while you readjust to American expenses
- **Easy online management** — control everything from your phone or laptop

## Tips for Managing Your First Month Back

Your first 30 days back in the USA can be hectic. Here's how to optimize your phone service during this transition:

### Monitor Your Data Usage

After potentially using WiFi-heavy international plans, American data usage patterns might differ. Most Nexitel plans include tools to track your usage in real-time.

**Data management tips:**
- Download offline maps before road trips home
- Use WiFi at home, coffee shops, and campus for large downloads
- Stream music and videos at lower quality on cellular data
- Set up data alerts to avoid surprises

### Test Coverage in Your Key Locations

Spend your first week testing signal strength at the places you'll frequent most:

- Your home or apartment
- Your workplace or campus
- Favorite coffee shops and study spots
- Gym and recreational areas
- Friends' houses and hangout locations

If coverage isn't meeting your needs, Nexitel's flexible prepaid structure means you can switch networks (Blue to Purple or vice versa) without penalty.

## Special Considerations for International Students on OPT/CPT

If you're an international student on Optional Practical Training (OPT) or Curricular Practical Training (CPT), prepaid plans offer specific advantages:

- **No SSN complications** — easier approval process without extensive US credit history
- **Flexibility for uncertain timelines** — no penalties if your work authorization changes
- **Budget predictability** — know exactly what you'll spend each month
- **Easy cancellation** — no issues if you need to return to your home country unexpectedly

## Making the Switch: How to Transition From Your Study Abroad Phone Service

If you maintained international phone service during your studies, here's how to smoothly transition to US service:

### Before You Leave Your Study Abroad Country
- Download and save important contacts
- Screenshot or export any important text conversations
- Back up photos and data to cloud storage
- Check if your international phone is unlocked for US networks

### Upon Arriving in the USA
- Order your Nexitel SIM card to your US address
- Keep your international SIM temporarily for any remaining contacts or verification codes
- Gradually transition accounts and services to your new US number
- Inform important contacts of your new number
