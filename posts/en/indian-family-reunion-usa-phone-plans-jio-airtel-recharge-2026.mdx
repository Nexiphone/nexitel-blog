## Keeping Jio/Airtel/Vi Numbers Active While in USA

Here's a common concern: "If my parents use USA SIM cards, what happens to their Jio/Airtel/Vi numbers? Will they lose the number?"

Great news — you can keep their Indian numbers active without expensive roaming. Here's how:

### Understanding Indian Carrier Deactivation Policies

- **Jio**: Numbers deactivate after 90 days of complete inactivity (no recharge)
- **Airtel**: Typically 60-90 days, depending on plan type
- **Vodafone Idea (Vi)**: Usually 90 days of non-recharge leads to disconnection

### Strategy: Minimum Recharges to Keep Numbers Alive

Instead of expensive roaming, do small recharges to keep the numbers active:

1. Before leaving India, ensure there's a base plan active
2. Around day 30-45 of the USA visit, do a small recharge (₹100-₹200 plans available)
3. This resets the deactivation timer and costs a tiny fraction of roaming charges

## Practical Tips for Family Reunion Success

### Before Your Family Departs India

1. **Download essential apps on their phones while still in India:** WhatsApp, Google Maps, Uber (they can use these with USA SIM cards)
2. **Write down important USA numbers:** Your number, hotel, emergency contacts
3. **Take screenshots of their Jio/Airtel account details:** In case they need to check balance or recharge plans
4. **Enable dual SIM capability:** Many modern phones support dual SIM; they can keep their Indian SIM in slot 2 (turned off to avoid roaming) and USA SIM in slot 1

### Upon Arrival in USA

1. **Insert Nexitel SIM cards before leaving the airport:** Get them connected immediately
2. **Test calling and data:** Make a quick call to you, open Google Maps to confirm data works
3. **Connect to your home WiFi:** Show them how to use WiFi calling for best quality
4. **Set up NexiTalk:** Install the app and demonstrate how to call India

### During Their Stay

1. **Monitor data usage weekly:** Most phones have built-in data tracking; help older relatives understand it
2. **Use WiFi whenever possible:** At home, hotels, restaurants — this extends mobile data
3. **Set up WhatsApp calling:** Free calls to India over WiFi/data (but NexiTalk is better quality for longer conversations)
4. **Remind them to recharge Indian numbers mid-visit:** Set a calendar reminder for the 30-45 day mark

### Before Returning to India

1. **Switch SIM cards back:** Day before departure, swap back to Jio/Airtel SIM so they land with Indian connectivity
2. **Keep Nexitel SIMs:** If they visit regularly, these SIMs stay active for future trips
3. **Download important photos/videos:** Make sure everything is backed up before data runs out
