When family lives in another country, keeping their phone topped up can be one of the most useful things you do for them — and one of the most frustrating if you don't know where to start. A prepaid phone that runs out of credit means missed calls, no mobile data, and sometimes no way to reach the very people trying to help. International mobile recharge solves this by letting you add credit to a phone abroad directly from the US, usually in under a minute.

## What Is International Mobile Recharge?

International recharge (also called a top-up) is a service that sends prepaid credit or a data bundle to a mobile number in another country. You enter the recipient's number and carrier, choose an amount, pay, and the credit lands on their line — no physical scratch cards, no wire transfers, no waiting.

This matters most for the millions of families where one member has moved to the US for work or study while parents, a spouse, or children remain home on prepaid plans. Instead of asking them to walk to a shop and pay in local currency, you handle it instantly from your phone.

## How the Process Actually Works

The [Nexi Volt recharge service](https://nexitel.us/support) is built around a few simple steps:

### Step 1: Confirm the Number and Carrier

You'll need the recipient's full phone number in international format and the name of their mobile carrier. If you're unsure of the carrier, ask them — sending to the wrong network can delay or fail the top-up.

### Step 2: Choose Credit or a Data Bundle

Some recipients want general airtime they can spend on calls, texts, or data. Others do better with a fixed **data bundle** — often a better value if they mainly use WhatsApp, WeChat, or video calls. Pick whichever fits how they actually use their phone.

### Step 3: Pay and Confirm

Complete payment, double-check the number on the confirmation screen, and send. Most top-ups deliver within seconds to a couple of minutes. Keep the confirmation in case you need to reference it.

## Tips to Avoid Problems and Extra Costs

**Verify the number twice.** The most common failed recharge is simply a mistyped digit. Confirm the full international number with your family member before sending.

**Watch for promotional bonuses.** Carriers in many countries periodically offer bonus credit on top-ups (for example, "recharge $10, get 50% extra"). Timing a recharge around these promotions stretches your money further.

**Understand what the credit can be used for.** In some countries, promotional bonus credit can only be spent on data or on-network calls. If your family needs to call other networks, standard airtime may be the safer choice.

**Set a reminder for recurring needs.** If you find yourself topping up the same phone every month, a simple calendar reminder prevents the line from lapsing at a bad moment.
