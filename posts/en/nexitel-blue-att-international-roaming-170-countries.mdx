## One Plan for Home and Travel

Most prepaid plans in the US do one thing well: domestic service. When you travel internationally, you're typically stuck paying for a separate travel SIM, buying an add-on that expires after a few days, or racking up roaming charges. Nexitel Blue is designed to handle both — affordable everyday US service on AT&T's network, with international roaming already built in across 170+ countries.

## What Is Nexitel Blue?

Nexitel Blue is a prepaid wireless service running on AT&T's network. Like Nexitel Purple (which uses T-Mobile), Blue is offered by Nexitel as an MVNO — meaning you get AT&T's infrastructure at a lower cost. What sets Blue apart is its international roaming capability, which is included rather than bolted on as an expensive add-on.

Full plan details are at [nexitel.us/blue-plans](https://nexitel.us/blue-plans).

## Why AT&T's Network Matters

AT&T operates one of the two largest wireless networks in the United States. For Blue subscribers, this means:

- Strong coverage in cities and major suburban markets
- Reliable voice and data in areas where T-Mobile coverage can be thinner
- Established international roaming partnerships built on AT&T's global agreements

If you live or work in a region where AT&T has historically stronger signal than T-Mobile, Blue will give you better day-to-day performance than T-Mobile-based alternatives.

## International Roaming in 170+ Countries

The standout feature of Nexitel Blue is international roaming coverage. When you travel, your phone stays on the same number and plan — no need to buy a local SIM at the airport or pay for an international day pass.

Roaming is available in over 170 countries, covering most of:

- **Europe** (UK, France, Germany, Spain, Italy, and more)
- **Latin America** (Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, Argentina, and more)
- **Asia** (Japan, South Korea, India, Philippines, China, and more)
- **Middle East and Africa** (UAE, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, and more)

This is particularly valuable for frequent travelers, business travelers, and immigrants who visit their home countries regularly. Instead of managing two SIMs or buying a new plan for every trip, your Blue plan works when you land.

## How It Compares to Carrier Travel Add-Ons

The major carriers — AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile — all sell international travel add-ons, but these typically cost $10–$15 per day and are limited in duration. A 10-day trip could add $100–$150 to your bill just for data roaming.

Nexitel Blue's included roaming avoids this entirely. For anyone who travels internationally even once or twice a year, the cumulative savings are significant.

### vs. Cricket Wireless
Cricket is AT&T's own prepaid brand and uses the same network. However, Cricket's international plans are limited and typically require separate add-ons for roaming. Nexitel Blue includes international roaming in the base plan.

### vs. Mint Mobile
Mint Mobile runs on T-Mobile and offers some international data at reduced speeds, but its roaming coverage and speeds are limited compared to Blue's AT&T-backed agreements.

### vs. Google Fi
Google Fi has solid international coverage but uses multiple networks and charges more per month for comparable domestic service. Blue offers a lower monthly cost for users who primarily need AT&T coverage domestically and standard roaming abroad.
