## Arriving in America for Graduate School? Here's Your Complete Phone Plan Guide

Congratulations on your admission to a US graduate program! As you prepare for your F-1 visa journey, one critical question often gets overlooked: how do you stay connected in America while keeping your China Mobile, China Unicom, or China Telecom number active back home?

This comprehensive guide covers everything Chinese graduate students need to know about US phone plans, international calling, and managing your Chinese mobile account from abroad.

## Why Chinese Grad Students Need a Different Approach

Unlike tourists visiting for two weeks, you'll be in America for 2-5+ years. Your communication needs are unique:

- **Affordable long-term service** — tourist SIM cards and international roaming are too expensive
- **Reliable coverage** — you need service on campus, in your apartment, and across the city
- **Calling China regularly** — staying in touch with parents, grandparents, and friends back home
- **Keep your Chinese number** — for banking apps, WeChat verification, Alipay, and important contacts
- **Budget-conscious** — grad student stipends require careful money management

## The China Mobile/Unicom/Telecom Dilemma

Many Chinese students make this mistake: they cancel their China Mobile, China Unicom, or China Telecom number before leaving. Then they discover:

- Their WeChat account needs SMS verification
- Chinese banking apps require the original phone number
- Alipay and Taobao accounts are locked
- They can't receive important verification codes
- Their parents' only contact number is now invalid

**The solution?** Keep your Chinese number active with minimal monthly recharge while getting an affordable US plan.

## Common Questions from Chinese Graduate Students

### "Can I use my Chinese phone in America?"

Yes! Most modern Chinese smartphones work on US networks:
- **China Mobile phones:** Usually support international bands
- **Xiaomi/OPPO/Vivo:** Most models work on T-Mobile/AT&T
- **Huawei:** Newer models work but may lack Google services
- **Check compatibility:** Ensure your phone supports LTE bands 2, 4, 12, 66 (T-Mobile) or 2, 4, 12, 17 (AT&T)

### "Should I get an iPhone in America?"

Many Chinese students buy iPhones in the US because:
