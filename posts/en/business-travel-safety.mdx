## Pre-Trip Safety Planning

Before you even pack your bags, proper planning can significantly reduce your risk during business travel.

### Research Your Destination

Take time to understand the safety landscape of your destination. Check travel advisories from the State Department, research neighborhood safety ratings near your hotel, and identify the location of the nearest embassy or consulate. Understanding local emergency numbers and common scams in the area can help you stay vigilant.

### Share Your Itinerary

Always ensure someone back home has your complete travel itinerary, including flight details, hotel addresses, meeting locations, and contact information. This simple step can be lifesaving in an emergency.

Create digital copies of your passport, driver's license, travel insurance, medical information, and emergency contacts. Store these securely but make sure they're accessible if your physical documents are lost or stolen.

## Hotel and Accommodation Safety

Your temporary home away from home requires special attention to security protocols.

### Choose the Right Hotel

Select accommodations with strong security features: 24-hour front desk service, keycard access, well-lit parking areas, and in-room safes. Read recent reviews that mention safety and security. Request a room on floors 3-6, which are high enough to deter break-ins but low enough for fire department ladders to reach.

Once you've checked in, use the safety timer feature in **One Tap Alert** when you're settling in for the night. If you're traveling alone and feel uneasy, set a timer for your expected sleep duration. If you don't check in when the timer expires, your emergency contacts will be automatically alerted with your location.

### Secure Your Room

When you arrive at your room, check that all locks work properly, including the deadbolt and chain lock. Use the peephole before opening the door, and use all available locks when you're inside. Never advertise that you're alone—leave the TV or radio on when you're out to suggest occupancy.

### Be Discreet About Your Schedule

Avoid discussing your detailed schedule in public areas where others might overhear. Don't announce to the lobby that you'll be out all day in meetings or that you're traveling alone.

## Transportation Safety Strategies

Getting from point A to point B safely requires vigilance and smart choices.

### Use Trusted Transportation Services

Stick with licensed taxis, verified rideshare services, or company-arranged transportation. Always verify the vehicle and driver match the app details before getting in. Avoid unmarked vehicles or drivers who approach you unsolicited.

When using rideshare services, the press-and-hold SOS button in **One Tap Alert** provides an extra layer of security. If you feel uncomfortable during a ride, you can discreetly hold the button for just one second to instantly alert all your emergency contacts with your live location, without having to unlock your phone or navigate through menus.

### Stay Alert During Transit

Sit in the back seat of taxis and rideshares, and keep your belongings close. Don't fall asleep during transport, and keep your phone charged so you can call for help if needed. Share your route with colleagues or family members when traveling alone, especially at night.

### Walking Safety

If you need to walk to meetings or dinner, plan your route in advance using well-lit, populated streets. Walk with confidence and purpose, stay aware of your surroundings, and avoid distractions like staring at your phone. Trust your instincts—if something feels wrong, it probably is.

## Meeting and Networking Safety

Professional events require their own safety considerations.

### Vet Meeting Locations

Before agreeing to meetings, especially with new clients or contacts, research the venue. Choose public, well-populated locations for first meetings. Be cautious about meetings scheduled at unusual times or locations that seem isolated.

If you must attend a meeting in an unfamiliar or isolated location, make sure someone knows where you are. With One Tap Alert's live location sharing, your emergency contacts can track your movements in real-time if you activate the SOS feature, providing an invisible safety net during potentially risky professional situations.

### Maintain Professional Boundaries

Be mindful of alcohol consumption at business dinners and networking events. Never leave your drink unattended, and be cautious about accepting drinks you didn't see prepared. If a situation becomes uncomfortable, don't hesitate to leave.

### Evening and Late-Night Events

Many business trips involve dinner meetings or evening networking events. These situations can create vulnerability, especially in unfamiliar cities. Always have a clear exit strategy, keep your phone charged, and let someone know your expected return time.

## Medical and Health Safety While Traveling

Health emergencies while traveling for business require special preparation.

### Know Your Medical Needs

Pack adequate prescription medications in your carry-on luggage with a few extra days' supply in case of delays. Carry a list of your medications, allergies, and medical conditions. Know where the nearest hospital or urgent care center is located near your hotel.

## Cybersecurity for Business Travelers

Protecting your digital information is as important as your physical safety.

### Secure Your Devices

Use strong passwords, enable biometric authentication, and keep your software updated. Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive business communications, or use a VPN if you must connect. Enable remote wipe capabilities in case your device is stolen.

### Protect Sensitive Information

Be cautious about what information you access or discuss in public spaces. Shoulder surfing in airports and coffee shops is common. Use privacy screens and be aware of who might be observing your device.

## Country-Specific Considerations

Different destinations require different safety approaches.

### Research Local Laws and Customs

Understand local laws that might affect you, including photography restrictions, dress codes, or behavior expectations. What's acceptable in one country might be illegal in another.

### Political and Social Climate

Monitor current events and avoid areas with civil unrest, protests, or political instability. Register with the Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP) if traveling internationally so the embassy knows your location.

### Language Barriers

Learn basic phrases in the local language, including how to ask for help or report an emergency. Have translation apps downloaded before you travel.
