## Best Prepaid Plans for Snowbirds and Part-Time Residents

If you split your year between two homes — say, a summer place up north and a winter spot down south — your phone plan should be just as flexible as your lifestyle. Postpaid contracts charge full price every month, even when you're abroad or settled in a vacation home with great Wi-Fi. Prepaid plans give snowbirds the freedom to pay only for what they actually use.

---

## Why Snowbirds Should Choose Prepaid

Many seasonal residents are still locked into expensive postpaid plans designed for full-time, single-location life. Prepaid solves this with:

- **Month-to-month flexibility** — no early termination fees
- **Lower base price** — plans starting at **$6/mo** save real money over a year
- **Easy pause and restart** — drop down to a low tier during light-use months
- **Nationwide coverage** — same SIM works in Florida, Arizona, Maine, or Montana

You're not locked in for 24 months — and you're not paying for premium data when you're playing pickleball at the beach.

## How to Build a Snowbird-Friendly Plan

### 1. Pick a "primary" line
Use one number year-round so family and doctors can always reach you. Keep this on a steady mid-tier plan.

### 2. Add a low-tier "secondary" line if needed
Some couples want one phone for emergencies at the second home. A second prepaid line at **$6–10/mo** is cheaper than a landline.

### 3. Use Wi-Fi calling everywhere
Both Blue and Purple support Wi-Fi calling. As long as your seasonal home has internet, you'll have full call quality even with weaker cellular signal.

### 4. Add NexiTalk for international calls
Many snowbirds travel out of the country during shoulder seasons. [NexiTalk VoIP](https://nexitel.us/nexi-talk) gives unlimited international calling from just **$4.99/mo** — perfect for staying in touch with family overseas.

## Easy Setup for Two Homes

- **Use eSIM where possible** — switch networks without waiting for a SIM in the mail
- **Bring your own phone** — no need to buy new hardware
- **Keep your number** — port from your old carrier for free
- **Update your address** if you spend more than half the year at one location, but the same SIM works in both

[Nexi Volt](https://nexitel.us/nexi-volt) is also handy for snowbirds who help support family abroad — send mobile recharge to 150+ countries directly from your phone.

## Tips for Saving All Year

- **Match your plan to the season** — drop to a lower tier during low-travel months
- **Use Wi-Fi heavily at home** — most snowbird homes have great broadband
- **Re-evaluate every 6 months** — prepaid means you can change with no penalty
- **Avoid international roaming** — use Wi-Fi calling or NexiTalk instead
