## What Indian OPT Students Actually Need in a USA Phone Plan

### Network Coverage That Works Everywhere

Your job search isn't limited to one city. You might interview in San Francisco, have a final round in Austin, and attend an onsite in New York. Your phone plan needs to work reliably across the country—not just in major metros.

**Essential requirements:**
- **Nationwide 4G LTE/5G coverage** for video interviews and LinkedIn browsing
- **Strong signal in university towns** where many OPT students start their search
- **Reliable service in tech hubs** like Bay Area, Seattle, Austin, Boston, NYC
- **No roaming charges** between states (many international plans charge for this)

### Enough Data for Job Applications and Interview Prep

During OPT job search, you're constantly online:
- Uploading resumes to company portals (often 2-5 MB PDF files)
- Researching companies on Glassdoor and Blind
- Taking LinkedIn Learning courses to upskill
- Attending virtual career fairs and webinars
- Video interviews on Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet
- Checking email dozens of times daily for responses

**Recommended minimum:** 5-10 GB/month for active job searching. Heavy researchers and video interview candidates may need 15-20 GB.

### A Stable USA Number for Recruiter Calls

This seems obvious, but many Indian students make critical mistakes:
- Using Google Voice (recruiters can't always call back)
- Relying on Skype numbers (poor call quality, dropped calls)
- Keeping only an Indian +91 number (expensive for US companies to dial)
- Using temporary/prepaid SIMs that expire (losing contact with recruiters)

**You need a stable USA number** that will be active throughout your OPT period and beyond. Recruiters will save this number, reference check companies will call it, and your future employer's HR will use it for onboarding.

### Affordable Plans That Fit OPT Budget Constraints

Let's be honest about OPT finances: you're likely living on savings, maybe a part-time CPT job ended, and you're waiting for that first paycheck. You can't afford ₹8,000/month (roughly $95-100) phone plans right now.

Many students are paying $40-70/month with major carriers, locked into annual contracts, or dealing with hidden fees. During a 3-6 month job search, that's $240-420 you could use for interview travel, professional clothes, or certification courses.

## Staying Connected to India: The Jio/Airtel/Vi Challenge

Here's a problem every Indian OPT student faces: **you need to keep your Indian mobile number active** even while in the USA.

### Why You Can't Let Your Indian Number Expire

Your Indian mobile number is tied to critical accounts and services:
- **Banking OTPs** for ICICI, HDFC, SBI, Axis Bank transactions
- **UPI payments** linked to your phone (PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm)
- **Aadhaar authentication** for various government services
- **WhatsApp account** verification (losing number = losing chat history)
- **Professional network** – colleagues, professors, references still use this number
- **Family emergencies** – parents and relatives calling you directly

Many students assume WhatsApp calling is enough, but then face disasters:
- Bank blocks their account because they can't receive OTP
- Can't complete DigiLocker verification for document access
- Lose WhatsApp account when number is reassigned to someone else
- Miss important calls from family because they only have Indian number
