## What Is Bill Shock?

Bill shock is that awful moment when you open a phone bill and see a number that is double, triple, or ten times what you expected. It is such a widespread problem that the FCC has specifically named and tracked it for over a decade. The culprits are almost always the same:

- International roaming charges
- Overage data fees
- Third-party premium SMS subscriptions
- Device financing that quietly turned into a loan
- Promotional price expiring after 12 months

If any of those sound familiar, prepaid is your way out.

## Top Causes of Bill Shock

### 1. Roaming Charges Abroad

A single week in Mexico on an unprepared postpaid line can add $200+ to a bill. Always check your plan's international policy before you travel, or use [NexiTalk VoIP](https://nexitel.us/plans) for cheap calls.

### 2. Data Overages

Many "unlimited" postpaid plans are not truly unlimited – they throttle or charge for each extra GB. Prepaid plans have a fixed ceiling: when the data is gone, you simply slow down or buy a top-up.

### 3. Premium SMS Scams

Text messages from "horoscope clubs" or "quote of the day" services can silently add $9.99/mo to a postpaid bill. Prepaid lines usually block these by default.

### 4. Device Financing

Flagship phones split into 24 monthly payments feel cheap, but they are still a $1,000 loan. Miss a payment and fees start. Prepaid lets you bring your own phone and avoid the loan entirely.

### 5. Promo Pricing Expiring

That "$30/mo" promo can quietly become "$85/mo" after month 12. With [Nexitel plans](https://nexitel.us/plans) the price you see is the price you pay, month after month.
