## Understanding Your Dual-Number Options

### Physical Dual SIM

Traditional dual SIM phones have two physical SIM card slots, allowing you to insert two separate SIM cards. This method works with:

- Most Android devices from Samsung, Google Pixel, OnePlus, and Motorola
- Select international iPhone models (though US iPhones typically don't have dual physical SIM slots)
- Budget-friendly smartphones that support dual standby or dual active modes

### eSIM + Physical SIM (Most Popular in 2026)

The most common setup in the US combines one physical SIM card with an eSIM (embedded SIM). This technology is built into:

- iPhone XS and newer models
- Google Pixel 3 and newer
- Samsung Galaxy S20 and newer
- Most flagship smartphones released after 2022

With this configuration, you can activate one [Nexitel Blue plan](https://nexitel.us/blue-plans) on your physical SIM and a [Nexitel Purple plan](https://nexitel.us/purple-plans) on your eSIM, giving you coverage on both AT&T and T-Mobile networks.

### Dual eSIM

Newer devices in 2026 support multiple eSIM profiles, allowing you to run two eSIM lines simultaneously without any physical SIM card. The iPhone 14 and newer US models, for example, are eSIM-only and can maintain two active eSIM numbers.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Data Not Working on Secondary Line

Ensure you've selected the correct line for cellular data in your settings. Most phones only allow one line to use mobile data at a time, though you can switch between them.

### Calls Going to Wrong Number

Check your default line settings and contact-specific preferences. You can manually select which line to use before making each call if needed.

### eSIM Activation Problems

Make sure you're connected to WiFi during eSIM setup. If the QR code won't scan, check for a manual activation code option in your Nexitel account portal.

### Poor Signal on One Line

With Nexitel's dual-network advantage, you can switch which line uses data based on signal strength in your area. If your Blue plan has weak signal, switch to your Purple plan's data.
